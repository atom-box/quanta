Love remembering things?

The trick for remembering things is to manipulate them 3, 4, or more times before the test.

Zuntum is a way to learn Cornell note taking on your phone or tablet. I was converted to Cornell note-taking by the public school teachers in AVID at Madison East High School .

Born in the Ivy League

Called 'Cornell Notes' since it was promoted by a professor at Cornell College in the 1940s.

Used by AVID

A network of 700 public & private schools based in San Diego, California.

Two-column format

Write your notes on the right and your quiz questions on the left.

Works for reading assignments

Coming soon: Import PDFs and online articles.

Free

Your feedback is my only payment. Drop me a comment to let me know what you use it for.

Private

This site will never sell your metadata, anonymized or not, nor your email address.

Portable

What's easier to carry, your phone or a notebook?

Part of your broader toolkit

A nice complement to other review methods like concept-mapping, annotation, flashcards, and outlining.

Beyond note-taking

Generating questions, ideas and summaries, forces you to actually think about the concepts

Quick

The most common routine is to study from these notes 10-15 minutes each day

Small bites

Forces you to study in tiny bursts, not a giant cram session.

Sneaky

When your teacher sees you with your phone remind them you're studying.
